1. Introduction

Welcome to TenAIs Studio ("we", "us", "our"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use the TenAIs Studio mobile application (Android & iOS) and its associated backend services (collectively, "the App").

TenAIs Studio is a hackathon management platform that enables participants, coordinators, judges, and administrators to organize, participate in, and evaluate hackathon events. The App facilitates team formation, project submissions, judging, leaderboards, and related collaboration features.

Effective date: February 10, 2026. By creating an account or using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the App immediately.

7. Cloud Storage & File Uploads

Files you upload (avatars, hackathon banners, submission documents and images) are stored in Microsoft Azure Blob Storage. Specifically:

  • Each file receives a unique identifier (UUID) and is stored with its original content type.
  • Access to files is controlled via time-limited Shared Access Signatures (SAS tokens) — URLs expire and cannot be used indefinitely.
  • Storage containers are private by default; files are not publicly indexed.
  • When you request account deletion, all files associated with your account are queued for deletion from cloud storage.

8. Data Security

We implement multiple layers of security to protect your data:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transferred between the App and our servers uses HTTPS/TLS encryption.
  • Password security: Passwords are never stored in plaintext. They are irreversibly hashed using industry-standard algorithms before storage.
  • OTP & token hashing: One-time passwords and password reset tokens are hashed with SHA-256 before storage — we never store the raw values.
  • JWT authentication: Access tokens are short-lived. Refresh tokens are hashed and stored server-side with device association (IP + user-agent) and are automatically invalidated upon expiry.
  • On-device security: Authentication tokens are stored in Expo SecureStore (hardware-backed encrypted storage on supported devices).
  • Rate limiting: API endpoints are protected by rate limiters (e.g., login attempts are capped at 5 per 15 minutes per IP+email combination) to prevent brute-force attacks.
  • Role-based access control: Server-side authorization ensures users can only access data and actions permitted for their role (participant, coordinator, judge, admin).
  • Input validation: All user inputs are validated and sanitized on the server to prevent injection attacks.
  • Automatic token expiry: Expired refresh tokens and OTPs are automatically purged from the database via TTL (Time-To-Live) indexes.
While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal data, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

To exercise any right not available directly through the App, please email us at support@tenais.co.in. We will respond within 7-14 days.

11. Account Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your account at any time by emailing support@tenais.co.in. Upon account deletion:

  • Your personal data (name, email, phone, profile details, social links, education, experience, skills, interests, contact details) is permanently erased.
  • Your uploaded avatar and files are deleted from cloud storage.
  • All active sessions (refresh tokens) are revoked and removed.
  • Your user references in teams, submissions, and evaluations are replaced with a "Deleted User" placeholder to maintain data integrity of hackathon records without retaining your identity.
  • Notifications associated with your account are removed.
How to delete your account: Send an email to support@tenais.co.in from the email address associated with your account, with the subject line "Account Deletion Request". Please include your registered name for verification. We will confirm receipt and process the deletion within 7-14 days.
